Time-Based Fraud Patterns

Fraudulent transactions often exhibit temporal anomalies that distinguish them from legitimate activity.

Suspicious Time Windows:
- Late night transactions (1 AM - 5 AM local time): 80% anomaly score
- Transactions during user's historically inactive hours
- Transactions that occur at times inconsistent with the merchant's operating hours

Time-Based Features:
- time_anomaly: Binary/continuous score based on transaction hour
  - Hours 1-5: Score 0.8 (high anomaly)
  - Hours 6-23: Score 0.0 (normal)
- Day-of-week patterns: Weekend vs weekday spending patterns
- Holiday anomalies: Unusual activity during holidays

Temporal Velocity:
- Multiple transactions within 1 minute: likely automated
- Transactions every 5-10 minutes for an extended period: pattern suggests compromise
- Transaction clusters followed by long gaps: test-then-exploit pattern

Geographic-Temporal Impossibility:
- Transactions in two distant locations within an impossible travel time
- Example: Transaction in New York at 2 PM, then Tokyo at 2:30 PM (physically impossible)

Seasonal Patterns:
- Holiday season: Higher baseline for false positives
- Tax season: Unusual high-value transactions may be legitimate
- Back-to-school: Increased e-commerce activity
