High-Value Transaction Fraud

High-value transaction fraud involves unusually large financial transactions that deviate significantly from a user's normal spending patterns.

Key Indicators:
- Transaction amount exceeds 3 standard deviations from the user's mean
- Transaction amount is above $5,000 for typical consumer accounts
- Single transaction that consumes more than 50% of remaining daily limit
- Multiple high-value transactions in quick succession (layering)

Risk Scoring:
- Amount normalized above 0.7 (relative to max observed) contributes significantly to fraud score
- Combined with high velocity (multiple transactions in under 10 minutes), this pattern is highly suspicious
- High-amount flag triggers additional scrutiny when combined with time anomalies

Common Fraud Scenarios:
1. Stolen credentials used for a single high-value purchase before the account is locked
2. Gradual escalation: small test transactions followed by a large withdrawal
3. Split transactions: multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds
4. Cross-merchant layering: high-value transactions spread across multiple merchants

Thresholds:
- Transactions above $1,000: medium risk flag (0.3 contribution)
- Transactions above $5,000: high risk flag (0.6 contribution)
- Transactions above $10,000: critical flag requiring manual review
