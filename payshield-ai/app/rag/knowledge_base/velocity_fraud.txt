Velocity-Based Fraud Detection

Velocity checks monitor the speed and frequency of transactions to detect automated or rapid-fire fraud attempts.

Transaction Velocity Patterns:
- Normal: 1-3 transactions per hour, 5-15 per day
- Suspicious: 5-10 transactions per hour
- Critical: More than 10 transactions per hour

Types of Velocity Fraud:
1. Card Testing: Rapid small-value transactions to test stolen card validity
2. Bot Attacks: Automated transactions at machine speed
3. BIN Attacks: Sequential card number testing using Bank Identification Numbers
4. Credential Stuffing: Rapid login attempts followed by transactions

Detection Features:
- frequency_1h: Ratio of transactions in last hour vs maximum threshold (10/hour)
- frequency_24h: Ratio of transactions in last 24h vs maximum threshold (50/day)
- velocity: Binary flag when hourly frequency exceeds 50% of threshold
- Inter-transaction time: Time between consecutive transactions

Scoring Impact:
- Velocity feature contributes 10-20% of overall fraud score
- When combined with amount anomalies, velocity patterns increase fraud score significantly
- Isolated velocity spikes may indicate legitimate bulk purchasing (needs context)

Response Actions:
- Soft block: Additional authentication required for next transaction
- Hard block: Account suspended pending review
- Alert: Notification sent to fraud team and account holder
