Account Takeover (ATO) Fraud

Account Takeover fraud occurs when a fraudster gains unauthorized access to a legitimate user's account and conducts unauthorized transactions.

Key Indicators:
- Login from a new device or unusual location
- Rapid change of account settings (email, phone, password)
- Unusually high transaction amounts immediately after account changes
- Multiple transactions to previously unknown merchants
- Transactions that max out daily or monthly limits

Behavioral Anomalies:
- Transaction velocity spike: more than 5 transactions per hour for a user who typically makes 1-2 per day
- Amount anomaly: transactions 3x or more above the user's average
- Time anomaly: transactions at hours when the user has never been active
- Geographic anomaly: transactions from locations far from user's typical area

Prevention Measures:
- Multi-factor authentication enforcement
- Session monitoring and anomaly detection
- Device binding and trust scoring
- Behavioral biometrics analysis
